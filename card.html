Gray Wolf

The sounds of a Gray wolf howl can carry as far as 10 miles, because of the high pitch.

  • Scientific name: Canis lupus
  • Height: 80 – 85 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)
  • Eats: Arctic fox
  • Speed: 50 – 60 km/h (Running)
  • Mass: 30 – 80 kg (Adult), 23 – 55 kg (Adult)
  • Life Span: 16 years (In captivity), 14 years (In the wild)

The gray wolf (Canis lupus), is a large canine native to Eurasia and North America. More than thirty subspecies of Canis lupus have been recognized, and gray wolves, as colloquially understood, comprise non-domestic/feral subspecies. It is also distinguished from other Canis species by its less pointed ears and muzzle, as well as a shorter torso and a longer tail.
